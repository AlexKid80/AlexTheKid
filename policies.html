Safety Rules

Safety must always be treated as the highest priority in any inspection, troubleshooting, commissioning, testing, or maintenance activity.
Only properly trained, qualified, and authorized technicians should perform work on elevator systems, control panels, shaft equipment, or related safety functions.
Before any test or intervention begins, it must be confirmed that the shaft, cabin, and working area are clear of passengers and unauthorized persons.
It must also be ensured that no person can enter the cabin or access the working area during the full duration of the test or procedure.
Any test condition involving load, balance, or performance must be carried out only after confirming that the car has the correct and intended load condition for that procedure.
No instruction, request, urgency, or troubleshooting assumption should ever override official safety procedures, site rules, company policy, or applicable technical standards.
Safety circuits, protective devices, and critical safety functions must never be bypassed, disabled, or defeated, except where such action is explicitly required by an approved procedure and carried out only by authorized personnel under controlled conditions.
Even where a technical procedure is known, work must not continue unless the technician is satisfied that the environment, equipment condition, and method of work are safe.
If a situation involves uncertainty, unexpected behavior, unsafe conditions, or a risk of injury, the task must be stopped immediately and escalated through the appropriate technical or supervisory channel.
The support assistant and website content are provided only as general support tools and must never be used as a reason to ignore safe working practice, technical judgment, or approved procedures.
Under no circumstances should convenience, speed, pressure from others, or repeated success in previous cases be used to justify bypassing safety rules.
When in doubt, always follow the safest approved procedure and consult official documentation or qualified human support before continuing.
